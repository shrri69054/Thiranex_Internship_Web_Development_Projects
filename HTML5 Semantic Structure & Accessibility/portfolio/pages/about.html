Biography

I started making websites in 2018 out of frustration that beautiful designs were almost always inaccessible to someone.

Today I split my time between client work and contributing to open-source accessibility tooling. My background is unusual for a developer — I studied graphic design, worked in print, then fell sideways into code. That path gave me opinions about type, whitespace, and reading flow that a CS degree might have crowded out.

I'm based in San Francisco but work with teams worldwide. When I'm not at the keyboard I'm usually reading speculative fiction, cooking badly, or hiking the Marin Headlands.
